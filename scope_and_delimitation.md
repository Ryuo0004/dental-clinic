# Scope and Delimitation

The Myles Dental Clinic Management System is a web‑based application designed to streamline daily clinical operations through three coordinated dashboards for Administrators, Dentists, and Patients. The system’s scope covers patient onboarding and profile maintenance, dentist scheduling and availability management, appointment planning and approval, treatment documentation, internal messaging, and basic inventory oversight. Patients can create and manage bookings, review upcoming visits, and communicate with the clinic. Dentists can view assigned schedules, register treatment outcomes, and collaborate with staff. Administrators supervise the end‑to‑end workflow, ensuring that process rules, records, and user access are consistently enforced across modules.

Within this functional scope, the system provides core features including patient registration and profile management; appointment creation by patients with administrative approval/confirmation, rescheduling, cancellation, and completion tracking; and structured treatment records captured as completed appointments. Dentist scheduling includes availability toggling and leave marking on specific dates, with the option to cancel affected appointments. Inventory management supports tracking of supplies and consumables at a basic level to aid replenishment decisions. The platform further offers operational summaries and lightweight reporting (e.g., counts, filtered lists, and status badges) that assist in monitoring workload, pending actions, and daily activities.

Administrative privileges extend to user management (e.g., adding patients without self‑signup, activating/inactivating accounts, role‑based access), configuration of clinic settings (branding, timezone, hours), and activity oversight through appointment status transitions, notes, and message logs. Administrators can update dentist availability, manage appointment lifecycles, record administrative notes, and moderate patient communications to maintain data quality and service consistency. These controls are implemented with attention to secure handling of patient and operational data, using role separation and server‑side validation to preserve confidentiality and integrity.

The system’s delimitations clarify that advanced financial, insurance, and clinical features are out of scope for the current version. It does not implement online payment processing, insurance benefits verification, claims submission, or complex billing workflows. Comprehensive electronic medical records (e.g., diagnoses, prescriptions, imaging archives), advanced human‑resource scheduling (multi‑site rosters, payroll), and automated omnichannel notifications (SMS or push) are not included. Analytics are limited to essential operational reporting rather than business intelligence or predictive models, and the system does not provide full internationalization or specialized regulatory tooling. While email notifications and internal messages are supported, the platform prioritizes core operational efficiency over extended communication channels.

Overall, the Myles Dental Clinic Management System focuses on digital efficiency, user convenience, and secure data handling for routine clinical operations. By centralizing appointment flows, dentist availability, treatment documentation, inventory awareness, and administrative control within role‑specific dashboards, it provides a practical foundation for daily management while maintaining clear boundaries around non‑essential or high‑complexity capabilities. These delimitations enable a reliable and maintainable solution that can be extended in future phases as organizational needs evolve.
